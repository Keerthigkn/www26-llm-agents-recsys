LLM & Agents for Recommendation Systems

The Web Conference 2026 (WWW'26) ‚Ä¢ Dubai, UAE

Abstract

Recommendation systems underpin much of the web economy‚Äîfrom content feeds and e-commerce to advertising and social interaction. As Large Language Models (LLMs) and autonomous agents increasingly power these systems, we are witnessing a shift from centralized recommendation pipelines to agentic ecosystems that plan, negotiate, and interact across product discovery, personalization, and fulfillment.

LLMs introduce new capabilities for reasoning over multi-modal data, grounding recommendations in natural language, and enabling conversational, zero-shot personalization. Meanwhile, multi-agent systems unlock emergent coordination across buyers, sellers, and platforms‚Äîyet raise challenges of interpretability, robustness, and evaluation. Traditional short-term metrics like clicks and conversions fail to capture coordination quality, long-horizon trust, or fairness across stakeholders.

This workshop convenes the communities of recommendation systems, multi-agent learning, information retrieval, and mechanism design to define principles for transparent, scalable, and responsible agent-driven personalization. We invite work on architectures for LLM-integrated recommendation, agent collaboration, retrieval-augmented generation, reasoning and alignment, evaluation frameworks, fairness and trust, and real-world deployment case studies. By bridging academic insights with industry deployments, this forum seeks to shape the next generation of LLM and agent-based recommendation systems‚Äîsystems that are adaptive, explainable, and aligned with user and societal welfare.

Large Language Models (LLMs) have recently emerged as transformative tools in artificial intelligence, revolutionizing various domains. In recommendation systems, LLMs have demonstrated remarkable potential to drive innovation and enhance user experiences. Recommendation data typically includes multi-modal information, such as product titles, images, user-item interactions, and customer reviews. The richness and diversity of this data create unique opportunities for designing and applying advanced LLM models.

LLMs have already been applied to a range of recommendation tasks, including personalized content recommendation, conversational search, product classification, question answering, and contextual advertising. They have also been integrated into real-world production systems. Despite these successes, the adoption of LLMs in recommendation systems is still in its early stages. Significant challenges remain, such as ensuring the accuracy of LLM-generated content, improving efficiency, mitigating biases, and safeguarding user privacy and data security.

This workshop seeks to explore a wide range of topics related to the use of LLMs and agents in recommendation systems, encompassing methods, datasets, applications, and systems. We invite submissions that address these challenges and contribute to advancing the state-of-the-art in LLMs for recommendations.

Submission Guidelines

We mainly follow the WWW guidelines for paper submission except for a single-blind peer review. All important guidelines are as follows:

  • Single-blind peer review. There is no requirement for anonymous submission unless authors prefer.
  • Page limits: Full papers cannot exceed 8 pages, including an appendix, plus unlimited references (paper content is limited to 8 pages, which means that if you have an appendix, then it should be included within that page limit. It is also ok if you do not have an appendix and instead 8 pages of content). Short papers cannot exceed 4 pages plus unlimited references.
  • Formatting Requirements. Submissions must be in English, in double-column format, and must adhere to the ACM template and format (also available in Overleaf). Word users may use the Word Interim Template and the recommended setting for LaTeX is: \documentclass[sigconf, review]{acmart}.
  • Prior Publication: Workshop papers that have been previously published or are under review for another journal, conference or workshop should not be considered for publication.
  • Submission Format: Papers should be submitted in PDF format, electronically, using the submission system.

Submission Tracks

We emphasize that the topics list is not exhaustive and welcome submissions in any related areas. Besides regular research papers, though no need to specify your track during submission, we also welcome other tracks, including:

  • Demo Track, which discusses the designing details about LLM and agentic recommendation systems.
  • Applied/Industry Track, which investigates applied challenges in agents for recommendation systems.
  • Dataset/Benchmark Track, which introduces new datasets and benchmarks regarding agents for recommendation systems.

Topics of Interest

Agent Architectures & Foundation Models

  • Architectures for LLM-integrated recommendation (fine-tuning, adapters, distillation)
  • LLM agents for search, recommendation, and conversational negotiation
  • Tool-using assistants, memory-augmented agents, and long-horizon planning
  • Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) and knowledge grounding for recommendations
  • Multi-modal recommendation using LLMs (text, image, video, audio)

Multi-Agent Coordination & Learning

  • Multi-agent reinforcement learning for recommendation ecosystems
  • Mechanism design, incentive alignment, and strategic interactions
  • Credit assignment and coordination quality among agents
  • Agentic interaction loops between users, content, and LLMs
  • Multi-agent collaboration and reasoning in recommendation environments

Personalization, Alignment & Optimization

  • Reinforcement learning for personalization and long-term value
  • Alignment of LLMs with user intent and preference modeling
  • Human-in-the-loop learning and adaptive feedback systems
  • Latency, memory, and serving optimization for scalable deployment

Trust, Safety & Responsible Systems

  • Fairness, bias mitigation, and explainability in recommendations
  • Fraud and collusion prevention, regulatory compliance, and ethical governance
  • Privacy-preserving recommendation systems and responsible deployment

Evaluation, Benchmarks & Deployment

  • Evaluation frameworks: offline metrics, causal testing, and A/B experiments
  • Coordination and market simulation benchmarks
  • Off-policy evaluation and long-horizon value estimation
  • Open datasets, reproducible pipelines, and industry case studies
  • Orchestration frameworks, constrained RL/bandits, and system-level scalability
